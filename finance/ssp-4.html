FAQ — How to Use SimpleStockPicks

See Product Tiers →

Regime Score is a simple “market weather” label from our model: Hot, Warm, Icy, or Cold. It describes how clean or choppy the environment looks to the model — it does not tell you what action to take.

Trade / No-Trade is an internal stability flag. “Trade” means conditions look normal for the model’s setups; “No Trade” means the model sees unusual or unstable behavior. These are labels, not instructions or advice.

Conviction Score (High / Medium / Low) is the model’s clarity rating for a setup’s structure. It reflects how clean the pattern appears to the model — not how likely a trade is to “work”.

“Long” means the model sees upward structure; “Short” means the model sees downward structure. These are directional biases for context only. They are not recommendations to enter any trade.

Each idea includes a ticker, model direction (Long/Short), entry zone, stop zone, and target zone, plus a short explanation. It’s a structured snapshot of how the model sees that symbol at a point in time.

Entry Zone is where the model considers a setup “active” in its structure. It is a reference area only — not a signal to buy, sell, or do anything in your account.

Stop Zone marks where the model considers the structure invalidated. Many users treat it as a way to understand when the model believes a thesis is “broken,” not as a hard rule.

Target is the model’s projected completion zone for a move. It is a reference level that helps you see how far a structural idea extends — not a directive to exit there.

Our Story

SimpleStockPicks was built for people who are tired of hot takes and anonymous calls, but still want a structured way to learn how momentum trading can work. Instead of telling you what to buy or sell, we show you how a model frames ideas — then we record what happened next.

Every idea ships with zones, labels, and a short explanation. Wins and losses are logged side by side in a public diary. The purpose isn’t to be perfect — it’s to be honest, consistent, and educational enough that you can build your own judgment over time.
