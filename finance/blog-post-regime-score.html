Regime Score: A Simple Read on Market “Weather”

4-minute read · Educational only · Not investment advice

Before looking at any individual idea in StockBuddy or TraderPal, we like to start with one simple question: “What kind of market are we in today?”

The Regime Score is our way of answering that in plain English. It doesn’t tell you what to buy or sell. It just labels the overall environment so you know whether conditions look friendly, mixed, or rough.

What is the Regime Score?

The Regime Score is a daily “market weather” reading. Internally, we look at a bunch of signals (volatility, breadth, trend, news tone, etc.) and compress them into a single classification.

On your side, you don’t see the calculations — you just see a simple label like: Hot, Warm, Icy, or Cold.

It’s context, not a signal. You decide if and how you want to act on it.

The Four Regimes (In Plain English)

1. Hot

“Hot” means the market looks clean and supportive for momentum. Trends are behaving, breakouts are more likely to stick, and the tape feels risk-on.

  • Moves tend to follow through more often.
  • Pullbacks feel like dips, not disasters.
  • We generally prefer our bullish swing ideas in this environment.

2. Warm

“Warm” is our normal, everyday regime. The market is workable, but not perfect. Some things trend nicely, others chop around.

  • Still fine for ideas, just less ideal than Hot.
  • We expect mixed behaviour: some clean, some messy.
  • Pullbacks and headlines matter a bit more.

3. Icy

“Icy” means conditions are getting defensive or choppy. Things still move, but the tape is less forgiving.

  • Breakouts fail more often, reversals show up faster.
  • Index moves can feel whippy and frustrating.
  • We treat this as a “heads-up” to be more cautious and pay attention to downside risk.

4. Cold

“Cold” is our risk-off regime. Volatility, credit, or macro tone suggest the market is under stress or reacting to shocks.

  • Moves can be sharp, emotional, and news-driven.
  • Gaps and intraday reversals are more common.
  • We see this as a time to think more about defense, not offense.

What the Regime Score Tells You (and What It Doesn’t)

The Regime Score is designed to be simple and limited on purpose.

  • It does tell you: how friendly or hostile the overall environment looks.
  • It does tell you: whether today feels more like “go with trends” or “be more defensive”.
  • It does not tell you: what to buy, what to sell, or how much to risk.
  • It does not tell you: anything about your personal situation or portfolio.

It’s one layer of context you can combine with your own judgment, rules, and risk limits.

How It Fits With StockBuddy & TraderPal

In StockBuddy, you mainly see the end result: clear ideas with entries, stops, and targets. In TraderPal, the Regime Score sits above the ideas and gives you a quick snapshot of the market mood first.

Many users simply check the Regime label (Hot, Warm, Icy, or Cold) and the Trade / Caution / No-Trade note before they look at any setups. It’s an easy way to avoid treating every single day like it’s the same.

You don’t need to know how the Regime Score is calculated to use it. Just think of it as a quick “market weather” headline to read before you dive into the details.
