What Is the Regime Score?

The Regime Score is our daily “market weather” read. Behind the scenes, our system processes volatility, strength, trend, credit, and news tone — then compresses everything into one simple label:

Hot Warm Icy Cold

It’s context — not a signal. You decide how to use it.

The Four Regimes (In Plain English)

1. Hot

A clean, supportive environment where trends behave and momentum works well.

  • Moves follow through more often
  • Pullbacks feel like dips, not danger
  • Momentum ideas behave cleaner

2. Warm

The “normal” regime — workable, but not perfect. Some trends clean, some choppy.

  • Mixed trend quality
  • Pullbacks matter a bit more
  • Still a healthy environment overall

3. Icy

A defensive or unstable environment. Trends break more easily, reversals show up faster.

  • Breakouts fail more often
  • Whipsaws and chop increase
  • A cue to slow down and stay alert

4. Cold

A risk-off environment. Volatility or macro pressure makes moves sharp, emotional, and news-driven.

  • Sharp moves and wider ranges
  • Gaps and intraday reversals common
  • More about defense than offense

What the Regime Score Does (and Doesn’t) Tell You

The Regime Score is intentionally simple:

  • It does tell you how friendly or hostile the market looks.
  • It does tell you whether the environment feels steady or shaky.
  • It does not tell you what to trade.
  • It does not tell you anything about your own risk or portfolio.

It’s only one layer of context — you choose how to use it.

How It Fits Into StockBuddy & TraderPal

In StockBuddy, you see simplified weekly setups. In TraderPal, the Regime Score sits above everything to give you a quick “market mood” read before you look at entries, stops, and targets.

Most users check the Regime label (Hot / Warm / Icy / Cold) and the Trade · Caution · No-Trade note first — it keeps expectations realistic day-to-day.

Quick mental model: Hot/Warm = offense allowed (with your own risk rules). Icy/Cold = assume the market is working against you.

You don’t need to know how the Regime Score is calculated — just think of it as a daily weather icon for markets.
